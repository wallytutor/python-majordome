Welcome to Wally Tutor!

Over the past 10 year I have accumulated an important amount of teaching materials on the different fields of research I developped interest into. These were mostly applied mathematics, combustion, fluid dynamics, computational thermodynamics, ..., well you get it. For a few times I tried to contribute back to the community by sharing my study notes but lost motivation doing it alone. Recently we gathered a few folks to start sometime soon an YouTube channel and in this page I will provide links to the different subjects I have worked myself and some contributions from friends.

Everything we developped together for the YouTube channel will be made available directly in our Numericando profile, but some side track materials might still be linked to this personal page or to my curated study repository learning-scientific-computing. Notice that all materials from my old repository tutorials (and many others that are currently hidden because outdated or not good enough to be published) are being reworked and associated to the latter one until everything is migrated.

Because I am someone who learns by copying and modifying, many (not to say most) materials you will find in the repository are not of my own authorship, but relecture of others. Sometimes the materials are so deeply restructured that you would hardly say it came from a certain source. Nonetheless, I always keep reference to the original sources, generally through a link in the README file that goes in a certain folder. You might ask why I don't simply fork a project and perform my studies there. This is something I tried several years ago when starting my PhD and simply didn't work to me. I am strict about file naming conventions, folder structure, output files, paths, etc, so I focused at working with a single repository for everything and doing things my way.

If you find I infringed your copyright, please let me know before trying to sue me and I will promptly remove your references. If the license at the main level of the repository is incompatible with your desired level of licensing, I can place a compliant file at the level of the materials I have reworked from your repository .

I hope you enjoy my compilation and the materials I have autored myself. Since I am not an specialist on all the subjects I study, I cannot assure you I will always have answers to the questions you might ask me, but I will at least try to find the right person to help you. This is my view of how science should work, but sadly there are plenty of sociopaths out there.
